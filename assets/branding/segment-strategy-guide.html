Three Segments · One Signal · Version 1.0

This document defines the three core content segments of the GSE brand. Each segment has a dedicated color identity, purpose, and content strategy. Use this guide when planning posts, creating new assets, or onboarding collaborators to ensure consistency across all platforms.

Every segment exists within the same GSE universe — same visual language, same brand DNA — but each transmits a distinct signal to the audience.

// the three segments
// Segment 01 · Awareness
ON THE
RADAR
What to look out for — upcoming & trending
#FF2020
Neon Red

On The Radar is GSE's forward-facing awareness segment. This is where you spotlight games, news, and cultural moments that are gaining momentum — titles that are trending, generating buzz, about to release, or simply deserve to be on every gamer's radar before they break through. Think of it as the early warning system of the brand.

Primary Purpose
Surface games & news that deserve attention before the masses catch on
Target Timing
Pre-release, trending now, newly announced, or rising in popularity
Content Source
Industry news, upcoming release calendars, social trends, gaming forums
Audience Benefit
Viewers feel informed and ahead of the curve — not late to the party

// Segment 02 · Currently Active
LOADING...
What's loaded in right now — daily & core sessions
#00D4FF
Neon Blue

Loading... is GSE's live session segment — a window into what is actively being played on a daily basis. This segment is personal, present-tense, and grounded. It answers the question every viewer has: "What are you actually playing right now?" No hype, no previews — just the real, current playlist. Core titles, active campaigns, games mid-story, and daily drivers all belong here.

Primary Purpose
Showcase what is currently being played — present tense, active sessions
Target Timing
Today, this week — games that are loaded and in active rotation
Content Source
Personal game library, active saves, daily sessions, current campaigns
Audience Benefit
Builds community around shared games — viewers connect over the same titles

// Segment 03 · Newsletter Bridge
CHECK
POINT
A signal from The Frequency — stories worth sharing
#FFE033
Neon Gold

Checkpoint is GSE's newsletter bridge segment — a carousel-based reading experience that takes a story from the most recent issue of The Frequency and delivers it to social media audiences who may not yet be subscribers. It is an invitation, not a summary. Viewers get the full story here, but they also see what they are missing by not being in The Collective. The closing slide always converts to subscription.

Primary Purpose
Bring newsletter stories to social — grow The Collective subscriber base
Target Timing
After each newsletter issue ships — highlight one worthy story per post
Content Source
The Frequency newsletter — previous month's issue, high-relevance stories
Audience Benefit
Non-subscribers get real value — and are naturally led to subscribe for more
